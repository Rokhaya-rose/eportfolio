Black Box Investigation

By Rokhaya Mbaye

Background

In my technology class we are trying to figure out how a box called “The Mystery Black Box” works. When water is poured in this box, it comes out in surprising ways. Our goal is to figure out why that is and try to recreate it with our own model.

Claim

The mystery black box could have a system of separated containers that allows water to flow through.

Evidence

The Mystery Black box is a box that when water is poured in it comes out more or less than what we poured in. For example, when Mr Sinusas poured in 1000 ml 712 ml came out. And when he poured in 800 ml, 870 ml came out. It also took a while for it to come out. Our approach to figuring out how it worked was by sketching out our plan for recreating it. Our model's key function was to have it release water greater or less than what was poured in. We tested our model by pouring water in it and seeing if it was similar to the black box. Our results were that when we poured in 300 ml, 250 ml came out in the fourth trail. And when we poured in 200 ml we got 600 ml out on the last trail.

Reasoning Part 1

Models are a representation of a certain thing or topic. Models can be useful to help represent a physical form of a thought or ideas. Models are a great way to help visualize an idea in another perspective. But it also comes with some limitations. These include the inability to represent everything that is needed for the model. For example landscapes. It is also not one hundred percent realistic. Just like how our model isn't exactly like the mystery black box.

Reasoning Part 2

My evidence supports the claim I made because when we tested out my claim, it worked really well. You should believe my claim because it worked for our build. My evidence is convincing because our result came out like that of the mystery box.

Reasoning Part 3

Some may say that there is NOT a system of separated containers inside of the mystery black box. If that claim were to be true, then our results would not have matched the data from the original mystery black box. it would produce way too much water quickly or not enough water slowly. Our data did match both the timing and amounts observed from the real mystery black box, so this means that our claim could possibly be true. Another possible explanation was that there was a tipping bucket inside the mystery black box. If that were true, we should have observed how fast the water came out. Because we did not observe how fast the water came out, we can conclude there is not a tipping bucket in the real mystery black box.
